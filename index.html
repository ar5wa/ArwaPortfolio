Saviour, vr experince

Saviour is a virtual reality experience I created to raise awareness about how to respond quickly during a stroke emergency. The idea came from a deeply personal moment in my life — I witnessed someone close to me suffer a sudden stroke. In that moment, I felt helpless, unsure of what to do, and overwhelmed by fear. That experience stayed with me and opened my eyes to how critical every second is when a stroke happens. That’s where the idea for Saviour was born: an immersive, interactive experience that places the user in the middle of a high-stakes situation. The goal is to teach people how to recognize the early signs of a stroke (like facial drooping, arm weakness, and slurred speech) and take fast, life-saving action. Through this project, I wanted to combine technology with empathy — turning something I went through into something that could help others be more prepared and confident in an emergency.
